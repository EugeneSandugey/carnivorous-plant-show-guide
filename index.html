üåø Best Plants for Collectors

Beginner-Friendly

Rare & Specialty

  • Darlingtonia (Cobra Plant) - Challenging but rewarding
  • Utricularia (Bladderworts) - Tiny traps, beautiful flowers
  • Genlisea (Corkscrew Plants) - Underground traps
  • Rare cultivars & hybrids - Limited availability

üå± Care Reminders

Water

Use only distilled, reverse osmosis, or rainwater. Never use tap water!

Light

Most need bright light. Venus flytraps and Sarracenia need full sun.

Soil

Use nutrient-free mix: peat moss and perlite or sand. Never use regular potting soil.

Humidity

Most prefer 50-70% humidity. Use humidity trays or terrariums for tropical species.

Feeding

Never fertilize! They get nutrients from insects. Can hand-feed if needed.

Dormancy

Temperate species need winter dormancy. Research your specific plants' needs.

üì∏ About Past SCCPE Shows

What Makes SCCPE Shows Special

The SCCPE (Southern California Carnivorous Plant Enthusiasts) hosts what they call "the LARGEST and most IMPRESSIVE carnivorous plant show" in the region. Previous shows have been held at the prestigious Sherman Library & Gardens on Father's Day weekend, drawing over 1,000 attendees each year.

Typical Show Features

  • Judged Plant Competition - Expert judges award "Best in Show" prizes, sponsored by California Carnivores
  • Educational Presentations - Learn from experts about specific genera and growing techniques
  • Kids Activities - Children can earn free carnivorous plants through educational activities
  • Plant Sales - Thousands of plants available from multiple vendors

Notable Past Presenters

  • Jim of Booman Floral - 55+ years growing experience, manages the largest Green Swamp Genetics colony on the West Coast
  • Sandy Casey (Casey's Carnivores) - Pinguicula specialist known for propagation techniques
  • Frankie Obregon - Local legend specializing in Nepenthes hybridization
  • Sherri Patten - Expert on Australian sundews, especially the Petiolaris complex

SCCPE Throughout the Year

Beyond the annual show, SCCPE participates in:

  • Reptile Super Shows in Anaheim and Pomona
  • Southern California Spring Garden Show at South Coast Plaza
  • OC Plant Share events
  • Regular meetings at Balearic Community Center (1975 Balearic Drive, Costa Mesa)
  • Holiday parties with inter-club gatherings

üí° Expert Tips for Show Success

Timing Your Visit

  • Early Bird Entry (9 AM - $10) - Worth it for rare plants and best selection
  • Regular vendors set up by 10 AM sharp
  • Best plant selection typically gone by noon
  • Presentations usually scheduled 1:45-3:00 PM
  • End-of-day deals possible after 3 PM

Advanced Shopping Strategy

  • Make two passes: Scout first, buy second
  • Ask vendors to hold items while you complete your first pass
  • Rare cultivars and show plants sell within first hour
  • Bundle deals often available for multiple purchases
  • Join SCCPE ($20/year) for member discounts at vendors

What Veterans Bring

  • Small cooler with ice packs for heat-sensitive plants
  • Cardboard flats or boxes (sometimes vendors run out)
  • Plant labels and permanent marker
  • Hand sanitizer (after handling many plants)
  • List of your current collection to avoid duplicates

üåü Pro Tips from Regular Attendees

  • Network with other collectors - Many rare plants are traded/sold person-to-person, not at vendor tables
  • Ask about pre-orders - Some vendors take special requests if contacted before the show
  • Check plant health carefully - Look under leaves for pests, check root systems if possible
  • Temperature consideration - If it's hot, shop for tropical plants later in the day (they handle heat better)
  • Follow vendors on social media - They often preview what they're bringing to shows

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes to Avoid

At the Show

  • ‚ùå Impulse buying without research - Some plants need winter dormancy or special conditions
  • ‚ùå Leaving plants in hot car - Even 30 minutes can cook delicate species
  • ‚ùå Not checking for pests - Look under leaves and in pitchers before buying
  • ‚ùå Forgetting to ask about care - Each species has different requirements
  • ‚ùå Missing the presentations - Free expert knowledge you can't get elsewhere

Plant Care Mistakes

  • ‚ùå Using tap water - Will kill most carnivorous plants within weeks
  • ‚ùå Regular potting soil - Too many nutrients burn their roots
  • ‚ùå Fertilizing - They get nutrients from insects, not soil
  • ‚ùå Triggering Venus flytrap unnecessarily - Each trap only closes ~5 times
  • ‚ùå Keeping them too dry - Most need constantly moist soil
  • ‚ùå Indoor growing without enough light - Most need full sun or grow lights

Shopping Mistakes

  • ‚ùå Not bringing cash - Some vendors only take cash
  • ‚ùå No transport plan - Plants need secure transport home
  • ‚ùå Buying only tiny plants - Mix in some established plants for instant gratification
  • ‚ùå Ignoring dormancy needs - Temperate plants MUST have winter dormancy
  • ‚ùå Not joining SCCPE - $20 membership often pays for itself in vendor discounts

‚úÖ Setting Yourself Up for Success

Remember: These plants evolved in nutrient-poor environments. The #1 rule is to recreate their natural conditions:

  • Water: Distilled, reverse osmosis, or rainwater ONLY
  • Soil: Nutrient-free mix (peat moss + perlite or sphagnum)
  • Light: Most need lots of it - full sun or bright grow lights
  • Humidity: 50%+ for most species (bathroom windowsills work great!)
  • Patience: Let them catch their own food - they're surprisingly good at it
